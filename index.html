About

I work at the intersection of hydrology, remote sensing, and software. My recent work centers on the Great Salt Lake basin — characterizing the functional flow behavior of the streams that feed the lake and making those metrics explorable for researchers and water managers.

I like problems where the hard part isn't the model, it's the data: rare events, seasonal signals, leaky validation splits, and datasets that live behind cloud APIs. Most of my day-to-day is Python, geospatial tooling, and a healthy skepticism of my own results.

Projects

Great Salt Lake Functional Flows Explorer

ArcGIS Experience Builder · Utah State University

An interactive web app for exploring functional-flow metrics — peak flows, recessions, summer and winter low flows — across ~2,500 stream segments in the Great Salt Lake basin. Users pick a stream on a 3D map and see percentile tables of flow metrics by water-year type, plus hydrograph schematics comparing predicted and observed flow behavior at gaged sites.

  • ArcGIS Experience Builder
  • Feature services
  • Python figure pipeline
  • Hydrology

Satellite-Based Debris-Flow Detection

Machine learning · Google Earth Engine

Detecting when debris flows happened from space: a gradient-boosted model that reads multivariate time series of spectral indices (NDVI, NBR, BSI, NDWI) and flags event dates. The interesting challenges are rare-event class imbalance, time-aware cross-validation, and pushing feature engineering server-side in Earth Engine so iteration stays fast offline.

  • scikit-learn HGBT
  • Google Earth Engine
  • Spectral indices
  • Imbalanced time series
