About

I am a water-resources researcher interested in how rivers work, connecting hydrology, fluvial geomorphology, and aquatic ecology. Most of my work integrates field data collection, geographic information systems (GIS), remote sensing (e.g., Landsat, MODIS, Sentinel products), and statistical and machine learning models to answer critical applied research questions. Deliverables from my work include interactive web applications, novel GIS tools, reproducible analysis pipelines, and analyses supporting peer-reviewed research, all with the goal of helping water resource managers make science-based decisions about rivers in the Mountain West.

What drives me is pretty simple: rivers are among the most biodiverse and most degraded ecosystems on the planet, and there's real work to do conserving and restoring them. I want to be part of that work in a way that's both scientifically rigorous and practically useful.

I like problems where the hard part isn't the model, it's the data: rare events, seasonal signals, leaky validation splits, and datasets that live behind cloud APIs. Most of my day-to-day is Python, geospatial tooling, and a healthy skepticism of my own results.
